The Framework

Here's how it actually works.

01

Identify a problem.

Insights during exit interviews are too late. Find out the complaints before people feel they need to leave.

02

Ask “do I like this.”

“Do I like this?” only works when someone actually says it in the room.

03

Change one thing.

Small fixes compound. Make this part of your company DNA.

Most improvement starts as irritation. The discipline is saying it out loud, then doing something small about it.

The TL;DR

Turning decades into tl;dr

Most leadership books tell you to add things. More meetings, more tracking software, more oversight. IT taught me the opposite: speed isn't running faster, it's removing friction.

Find it, fix it once, and it stops being your problem. That's the heart of my book, Push Button Management.

Friction doesn't vanish, though. It lands on whoever's doing the technical work. That's the other book: It's A Hard Job.
